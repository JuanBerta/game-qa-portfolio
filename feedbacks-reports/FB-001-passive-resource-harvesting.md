# FB-001: Passive Resource Harvesting Loops via Placeables

## Overview
Right now, the Taming profession has a fantastic, distinct gameplay loop: you craft a trap, place and bait it, transport the trapped animal, and nurture it at a station over time using resources (food, water, potions) before RNG yields a domesticated animal.

I would love to see a similar mechanic expanded to other gatherable professions. We already have **Placeables** and heavy **Chunks/Objects** in the game; adapting them into a passive harvesting loop for professions like Mining, Forestry, and Fishing would beautifully bridge active wilderness gathering with town infrastructure.

---

## The Core Mechanics & Maintenance Loop
Instead of only hitting static nodes, players could craft high-tier, profession-specific placeables to deploy in their natural biomes.

To keep it balanced and engaging, these machines shouldn't just be "set and forget." They should mirror the nurture/care phase of Taming through a **Maintenance Step**, and follow a specific balancing rule: **they yield a solid, worthwhile bulk amount of resources, but always less overall yield per hour than manual gathering.** This ensures active playing remains the most rewarding method.

1. **Deployment:** You craft an item (e.g., a *Ferralith Drill* out of Ferralith) and place it in the correct biome.
2. **The Maintenance Phase (The Nurture Equivalent):** After a period of time, the machine requires player interaction to keep running. Instead of food or healing potions, players must supply it with **Fuel** (like coal or essence) or a **Repair Kit**.
3. **Extraction:** Once maintained, it finishes its cycle and yields a heavy object (e.g., a *Ferralith Ore Chunk*).
4. **Logistics & Processing:** The player transports the heavy chunk back to a settlement to process it at a **Mining Station** for a good bulk payout of resources.

### Profession Examples

| Profession | Placeable Item | Maintenance Requirement | Resulting Heavy Object | Processing Station |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mining** | Ferralith Drill | Repair Kits | Ferralith Ore Chunk | Mining Station |
| **Forestry** | Automated Logging Rig | Fuel | Massive Ironwood Log | Carpentry Station |
| **Fishing** | Deep-sea Crab Pot | Bait | Heavy Fish Crate | Fishing Station / Kitchen |

## Benefits for the Game & Economy

* **Standardizing a Proven Loop:** If players enjoy the anticipation of checking their traps, they will love checking their drills and nets. It makes the gathering ecosystem feel cohesive.
* **Preserving the Value of Active Play:** By capping the passive yield so that it gives a good amount but remains less efficient than manual gathering, active players never feel obsolete. It serves as a supplement for busy players rather than a replacement for active gameplay.
* **Stimulating Logistics and Trade:** Because these placeables yield heavy items (Chunks/Crates/Logs), players cannot simply teleport them away. It naturally generates a demand for transport infrastructure—like carts, ships, and player coordination—to move wealth from the wild back to community hubs.
* **Solving Resource Scarcity:** In highly populated areas, static resource nodes can be stripped fast. This system gives advanced players a way to invest their current high-tier materials into generating new resources without constantly competing over contested node spawns.
* **Creating New Crafting Demands:** The maintenance phase creates a constant economic sink for secondary items. Blacksmiths can craft Repair Kits, Cooks/Foragers can craft specialized baits, and Alchemists/Miners can supply fuel, ensuring multi-profession interdependence.
